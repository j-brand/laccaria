## Overview

Blackbeetle is my personal travel-blog and photo project. When my girlfriend
Isabell and I set off twice for the other end of the world — travelling across
Australia — we wanted to take family and friends along with us. Instead of
countless individual messages, it became one place where everyone can quietly
follow how we're doing and where we are: travel stories, pictures and small
galleries full of flowers, mushrooms and beetles.

## The challenge

The project had to be two things at once: a calm, image-led blog for our
families — and a tool we could use on the road, with little bandwidth, to manage
stories, galleries and media ourselves. Blackbeetle has grown with us over the
years; its first admin interface was still built in Angular.

## The approach

I built the front end with Vue.js — including a light/dark mode and a playful
landing page. Behind it sits a Laravel back end where all the content is managed:
stories, galleries and media files. I later rewrote the originally Angular-based
admin entirely in Laravel to make maintenance and operations far simpler. That
keeps Blackbeetle a living archive of our travels — and a place where our
families always know where in the world we are.
