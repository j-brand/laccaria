## Overview

Plan Shop is the fair-trade online shop of Plan International. Behind the
products — hand-embroidered cushion covers, woven scarves, bags and home
accessories — stand women's cooperatives around the globe: from the Peruvian
cooperative Sumaq Qara high in the Andes to seamstresses in India and Nepal.
Every piece tells a story of fair pay, good working conditions and economic
independence for women.

On this project I'm not working as a developer. Instead, I support the Plan
Shop team on the editorial and conceptual side: I plan the content, help bring
the stories behind the products to life, and propose new features and
improvements for the shop. It's project management with a heart for the cause.

## The challenge

A fair-trade shop lives on its stories — but the products still need to sell.
The art is in joining the two: making the origin and the social value of the
goods visible without losing sight of the e-commerce. On top of that comes the
task of keeping content planned and consistent across campaigns, collections
and seasonal moments.

## The approach

I support the team with editorial planning and coordinate the content for
stories, campaigns and collections. In parallel, I look at the shop from the
user's perspective and suggest concrete improvements and new features that
strengthen both the shopping experience and the visibility of the stories.
That way Plan Shop keeps growing as a place where good design, fair products
and strong stories come together.

> Products with an attitude need stories people love to pass on.
