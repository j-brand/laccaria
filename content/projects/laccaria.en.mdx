## Overview

laccaria.de is my own home on the web — a small, content-light marketing and
portfolio site that doubles as a proving ground for the stack I recommend to
clients. One rich landing page introduces who I am and what I do; the projects
section tells the story behind selected builds; and everything ships in both
German and English from a single codebase.

## The challenge

A developer's own site has to practise what it preaches. It needed to be genuinely
fast, fully accessible and bilingual without turning into a maintenance burden — no
CMS to babysit, no database to run, and no duplicated content trees drifting out of
sync between languages. At the same time it had to look distinct: a personal brand,
not another template.

## The approach

I built it on Next.js 16 (App Router) and React 19 with strict TypeScript. Content
lives as MDX with typed frontmatter — one file per locale per project — so case
studies read like documents, not database rows. Internationalisation runs through
next-intl with locale-segment routing; German is served unprefixed and English
under /en, with a shared message tree that keeps both languages honest. The visual
layer is a bespoke Tailwind CSS v4 design system — chamfered frames, a mono
eyebrow rhythm and a light/dark theme — with Swiper powering the featured-projects
slider. SEO metadata, hreflang alternates, the sitemap and JSON-LD are all derived
from a single origin, so there is nothing to hand-maintain.

> A site that practises what it preaches: fast, accessible and bilingual, with the
> content as plain files and nothing to babysit.
