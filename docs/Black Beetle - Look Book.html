Visual language

Warm, technical, cut at the corners.

Black Beetle pairs warm paper-and-forest tones with a chamfer — an angled corner cut instead of a rounded arc — for a quietly engineered feel. One look, two modes. Use the toggle, top right, to see dark.

Why I cut every corner (on purpose)

BB
Black Beetle
Jun 26, 2026

Rounded corners became the default because they're safe. Every product, every card, the same soft arc. Safe is forgettable — so I went looking for a corner that says engineered instead of friendly.

A chamfer is a decision. An arc is a default.

The cut is a single angled edge — cheap to draw, instantly recognizable, and it scales without the muddy in-between sizes a radius gives you. Pair it with warm paper tones and a forest-green accent and the whole system reads as quietly technical.
